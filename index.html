    Project Ideas


    After thinking thoroughly, I have come up with an idea for my project. This was formed base on RMIT student’s problem such as the rate of students participate in events and club. Before settling to Sai Gon campus, I was studying at Hanoi campus. The difference between these two campuses is the participation and student’s relationship. Despite the low in number of students at Hanoi campus, therefore the bonds between students was incredibly strong. RMIT itself does not operate any events but in facts they let the student council or others club decide which events will be held at school. So, this project will be focused on how to strengthen the bond of students and make them be more active at school events.

    As I have known, RMIT students use Messenger and Facebook as an intermediary to deliver announcement for upcoming events from student council or clubs. It seems that students must switch between these two applications consecutively, this action resulted in wasting more time or simply more annoying. This problem also occurs when you must go to Facebook to reply peoples who has comment on your post and then go back to Messenger to reply your club chat box or individuals for private chat. So, I have been thinking of making an app name ‘Let’s Connect’ as a solution for this problem.

    ‘Let’s Connect’ will be available on IOS and Android, the users which this project aim for is all students from every school but that’s the future goal, for now it just for RMIT students only. Similar to Canvas – a assignment announcement app, students have to use their school email to log in, if they instead using their own email rather than school email address, therefore they have to inform the name, school ID and RMIT code in order to participate in the forum. As I have mention above, these app will be accessible for other schools, so to provide privacy, students must type in the special code given by their school to log in, and each school will be display with clickable school icon so the users can distinguish easier. This quite like Facebook which is you must answer some question provide by the Page Headers and wait for they approvement to enter their community. When the students log in into their school forum a main page will shows up, every presenting or upcoming event will be displayed on this page followed by the date and time, on the head right corner there will be a menu icon for students to make a choice. The menu bar contains a magnifier icon for searching purposes such as events they want to see, a tent icon which refers to club, if students who have already joined a club this icon will ask they to type in the club name and lead to the chosen club forum, if that students have not joined any club so far and type in a club name, the systems will ask them to fill in the form, this form contain questions about the club and a time box where they choose date for the club interview. If they complete the form and click on the ‘Send’ button, this form will be delivered to the Club President and the participant will be informed the date and time for the interview that has been chose previously, there will be a mail icon at the top left corner to announce the users when there is a mail sent to them. When the students have been approved by the club president, their name will be listed in the club and they can be able to join the club forum. Furthermore, there is no limitation on applying for other clubs which will provide for the students more chance for achieving the approvement. When the user’s access to they club forum, there will be two option, the first one is for general group chat and the second one is for uploading the club events on the main page. When these new events were uploaded, and according on how many of them were published on the main page, the system will informed to all students in their school with a message such as “There are (number of events) new events, let’s take parts in these together!”, this announcement can be switch off on your phone setting.

    ‘Let’s Connect’ will be able to download on App Store and CH Play, which means is a mobile application. ‘Let’s Connect’ is free to download so what you need is a phone of your and get it from the store. Although ‘Let’s Connect’ is a mobile app downloaded from the phone specific store, therefore you can also access it from the Internet as well. If the user’s school is listed in the app, then the school itself should provide a website link which lead to ‘Lets Connect’ website. If they school does not have the website link, their can type in the app name on the searching engine such as Google, Bing or others to locate the website. Furthermore, there is no specific software, hardware or any relevant source tools that needed to access to ‘Let’s Connect’. For this project, is just an idea of mine, but if I want to make this project, there are some necessary requirements. Coding is the most essential skill for completing this project. Firstly, for IOS, there are three languages program that need to learn, and use is Swift, C++ and Objective-C. You can choose 1 out of these 3 languages to write the program. These 3 language programs are free to download. According to Apple, if you use Objective-C you have to get used to it before starting to code with it, but for Swift is much more easier as it a combination of some of the best features from C++ and Objective. Secondly, for Android and website, the only programming language that available for Android is Java, and for the website we could code them by using Atoms or some relevant software. Finally, if you having difficult in learning these language, there a website called MIT App Inventor which let you create an app on the phone by yourself, you can also create the app by downloading it from the phone. So, it very easy to find and download the software, but it is very important to get used to it before developing an application.

    If ‘Let’s Connect’ was completed, the bonds between RMIT students will be strengthen, and they will be more active at participating in school events and also joining clubs. Peoples besides acquiring knowledge, they will be making new friends as well, especially for new students. This project also helping the school to manipulate the information for the students. For example, some small events from other clubs never reach the students themselves because of other well-known events. So the impact that ‘Let’s Connect’ will help is to make all this events reach the students besides connecting the students together.
